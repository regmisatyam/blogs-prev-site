5G Speed: Unlimited Speed


1. 5G speed
The 5G network is a new wireless technology that uses millimeter waves instead of radio waves. These waves have higher frequencies than those used in 4G networks, meaning they travel faster. This means that data speeds should increase, but only if the network is optimized for the new technology.

2. 5G coverage
5G coverage is still relatively new, but it's expected to cover at least 100 million people by 2020. In comparison, 4G covers about 1 billion people today. That said, 5G coverage is likely to expand rapidly over the next few years.

3. 5G devices
5G-enabled smartphones are already available, including Appleâ€™s iPhone XS Max, Samsung Galaxy S10, LG G8 ThinQ, Huawei Mate 20 Pro, and Google Pixel 4 XL. Other 5G phones may arrive soon, including the OnePlus 8T, Xiaomi Mi Mix 3, ZTE Axon 10, and Nokia 9 PureView.

4. 5G apps
There are currently no 5G apps available, but developers are working on them. If you want to use 5G while traveling, you can download the 5G app from T-Mobile. You'll need to buy a compatible device and pay $20 per month for service.

5. 5G speeds
5G speeds are much faster than those offered by 4G networks. According to Verizon, the average download speed on its 5G network is around 300 megabits per second (Mbps). That's double the average download speed of LTE networks. AT&T says its 5G network offers peak speeds of 2 gigabits per second (Gbps) and average speeds of 500 Mbps. Sprint claims its 5G network offers average speeds of 1 Gbps.

6. 5G security
Security experts say 5G networks are less vulnerable to hacking than older technologies. However, some researchers warn that hackers could exploit weaknesses in 5G hardware and software to gain access to user information.

7. 5G safety
Experts say that 5G networks pose fewer risks to public safety than previous generations of wireless technology. But they caution that 5G signals could interfere with police scanners and medical equipment.
